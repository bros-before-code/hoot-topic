Slithering giants roam the wild — some longer than a bus! Here are the top 5 largest snakes ever recorded.

---

**1. Green Anaconda (_Eunectes murinus_)**  

<img src="/images/green-anaconda.jpg" alt="Green Anaconda" class="article-image">

> 📏 **Length**: Up to 30 feet  
> 🐍 **Where**: South America (Amazon Basin)  
The green anaconda is the **heaviest** snake on Earth. While it’s not always the longest, its massive girth and weight (up to 550 lbs) make it a true apex predator in swamps and rivers.

---

**2. Reticulated Python (_Malayopython reticulatus_)**  

<img src="/images/snake02.jpg" alt="Reticulated Python" class="article-image">

> 📏 **Length**: Up to 33 feet  
> 🌏 **Where**: Southeast Asia  
The reticulated python holds the title for the **longest snake ever recorded**. It’s a constrictor that can take down deer and pigs, and has a mesmerizing pattern that gives it its name.

---

**3. Burmese Python (_Python bivittatus_)**

<img src="/images/snake03.jpg" alt="Burmese Python" class="article-image">



> 📏 **Length**: Up to 23 feet  
> 🌎 **Where**: Southeast Asia, invasive in Florida  
Famous for its role in the pet trade and now as an invasive species in the Florida Everglades, this python is huge and highly adaptable. It’s powerful enough to eat alligators.

---

**4. African Rock Python (_Python sebae_)**

<img src="/images/snake04.jpg" alt="African Rock Python" class="article-image">



> 📏 **Length**: Up to 20 feet  
> 🌍 **Where**: Sub-Saharan Africa  
Africa’s largest snake is aggressive and muscular. Though not venomous, it has been known to prey on large mammals — and occasionally freak out villagers.

---

**5. Amethystine Python (_Simalia amethistina_)**  

<img src="/images/snake05.jpg" alt="AmethystinePython" class="article-image">


> 📏 **Length**: Up to 22 feet  
> 🌏 **Where**: Australia, Papua New Guinea  
Also known as the scrub python, this beauty can get massive. It lives in rainforests and is capable of climbing trees in search of prey like wallabies and bats.

---

> 🦉 *Fun Fact*: Despite their size, none of these snakes are venomous — they all use constriction to overpower prey.
